CW Whitney , EL Luedeling

Slides

Abstract

Decision makers are often faced with large uncertainties when planning development interventions. Given the extreme complexity of such decisions it is often difficult to forecast the likelihood of achieving the desired outcomes. Decision tools can offer a means to incorporate local knowledge about the diversity of interacting factors that can influence decision outcomes. They can offer decision makers information they need to best allocate resources to have the greatest impact. Hosted by the Freshwater Biodiversity Unit (FBU), IUCN Global Species Programme, with a brief introduction to the FBU’s work in the Lake Victoria Basin, East Africa, including work on the use and livelihoods value of freshwater biodiversity, which was completed in collaboration with the speaker.
