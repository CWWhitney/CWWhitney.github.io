I don’t usually write blog posts, but the **iNaturalist City Nature Challenge** has been too inspiring not to share.

Over the last few days in **Bonn**, I’ve been paying close attention to places most of us walk past without noticing: sidewalk cracks, edges of walls, and tiny wild front-garden spaces. What looked like almost nothing at first glance turned out to be small, complex ecosystems.

In several cracks and neglected corners, I found what felt like miniature “bonsai forests” — layered plant communities packed into just a few centimeters of soil and dust. I’ve already identified many species in these spaces, and each observation has made me more aware of how much life urban environments can support.

The challenge has reminded me that biodiversity isn’t only “out there” in remote landscapes. It’s also right under our feet in everyday city life, if we slow down enough to notice.

If you’re in Bonn (or anywhere), I highly recommend joining in: take photos, upload observations, and see your city with new eyes.
